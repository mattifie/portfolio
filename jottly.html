JOTT.LY product landing page

summary

During my Bloc apprenticeship, I was assigned the task of working with Jott.ly, a fictional SaaS web application startup. Jott.ly allows user to sign up, create an account, produce basic content, and collaborate with peers in editing, developing, and sharing content pages. My task was to develop Jott.ly’s branding and create a landing page for its first website.

problem

As a new startup, Jott.ly needed to establish a brand identity and effectively market its product to potential users.

branding & identity

Jott.ly’s product is founded on simplicity. The ease of use and straightforward concept of collaboratively creating and sharing content with peers is what would ultimately appeal to potential users. Its brand needed to mirror that simplicity in both design and content.

After conducting a mind-mapping exercise, I brainstormed and sketched logo concepts that I thought successfully exhibited Jott.ly’s functionality and accessibility. In correlation with being a writing-based product, my initial sketches integrated writing-related materials.

I landed on the typewriter button illustration and logotype combination, which was simple but still representative of Jott.ly’s core usage. In terms of color, I used cool blues to reflect the calm, fluid nature of the product and Jott.ly’s modern concept. Cool pink was used as an accent.

logo sketches & style guide

information hierarchy

The primary goal of Jott.ly’s landing page was to encourage website visitors to sign up and create an account. I created two low fidelity wireframe options. I found that a combination of both wireframe options resulted in the most effective information architecture.

low fidelity wireframes

Through varying text sizes, strategically placed calls-to-action, and concise, enticing content writing, I was able to structure a splash page that initially drew website visitors in and progressively lured them to sign up for the product. A strong hero image was also essential in instantly communicating Jott.ly’s brand and value. Proper padding between sections was integral in allowing users to absorb Jott.ly’s product descriptions and content.

high fidelity mockup

final product

view the live project

In developing the live landing page, I altered the color distribution slightly. Rather than overlaying each photo with a light blue, I chose to display the natural colors of the hero and footer photos; the photos' untouched appearance embodied a basic freshness illustrative of the Jott.ly brand and product.

Overall, this project was an essential exercise in marketing and content writing – two skills that are oft overlooked in the design world. As designers, our responsibility is to accurately embody the client's brand in a compelling way, one that resonates with users and ultimately incites action in associating with the product or brand. Solid UI is rendered somewhat useless if the content on the page isn't equally, if not more, engaging.
