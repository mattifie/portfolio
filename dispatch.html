DISPATCH mobile app branding

summary

Dispatch is a neighborhood-based city guide in the form of a mobile app. Dispatch works to connect patrons with neighborhood businesses, incentivizing their initial and return visits with a point and rewards system. Dispatch's ultimate goals are to increase neighborhood business and patronage, and demonstrate to city dwellers the diverse experiences these neighborhoods offer.

Although Dispatch had an established logo, tagline, and was in the midst of development, the app needed branding tools, specifically to generate social media excitement for its impending launch. Through researching the app's use cases and core user personas, I was able to create compelling images for social media posts.

problem

The app had not yet launched, but it still needed a solid brand and pre-launch marketing materials.

marketing objectives

As a first step in creating these images, I articulated Dispatch's marketing goals. The app required brand awareness, as well as the ability to draw consumers to download and use the app. Dispatch also wanted its marketing strategy to aid in forging partnerships with area businesses and tastemakers, as well as inspire key city players and businesses to vouch for Dispatch via positive social media testimony. Most importantly, Dispatch wanted to position itself as a city resource.

content tone

Before selecting and creating branding images, the app's founder and I worked to define Dispatch's content tone. We decided that content should be clean, personal, and human-oriented, as well as informational and welcoming. The tone should take on these characteristics in addition to a sense of honesty, one that embodies genuine customer service in the form of social media. Visuals should reflect these values – production of photos and videos will be clean, crisp, and bright – disseminating the idea that Dispatch has nothing to conceal and everything to give its consumers and partnering businesses.

The app's designer, Naomi Scheel, provided an App Store image (above image), which offered initial direction for the visual tone.

In making the following set of visuals, I used a combination of stock photos and my own, pairing each with gripping text and Dispatch's new tagline, "live like a local," which I also crafted.
