MARTA FIEWEGER ux designer

a story

For years now, my dad has been telling me to "pick a sport." I always wanted to quip back, "Well, dad, I play lots of sports. It's fairly difficult to pick one." These sports are both literal and figurative, as my interests and talents find their homes in many arenas.

At age six, I declared my life would be devoted to becoming a visual artist, mainly in the form of drawing and painting. While this declaration occurred early on, the path to the present is scattered with stints on stage, track and field victories, work in publishing, and time spent in the fashion industry. Although I was able to excel along the way, none of the aforementioned sports stuck.

I’ve come to view this range of experience as an advantage, especially in the realm of UX design, which feels like my natural focus for the following reason: it’s a bunch of sports within a sport. Design is human-centered; it requires strong writing skills and verbal craftsmanship; it hinges on expressing ideals, goals, and emotions in clean, enjoyable interactions and attractive aesthetics. A true designer works to convey a client's needs and wants for the purpose of providing his or her customers and/or users a truly sublime user experience.

UX Design is far more than the scrolling and swiping associated with modern web development. The goal of the browsing and swiping is what UX design facilitates, and while it involves a number of moving parts, its success is contingent upon fusing these parts to realize that end design goal.

While my relationship with design is just beginning, I believe that I can contribute by helping solve significant problems for my clients and peers, and do so with a marathon work ethic and perhaps the occasional [subjectively] funny joke.
