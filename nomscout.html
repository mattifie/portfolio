NOMSCOUT iOS 8 mobile app design

summary

NomScout is a project I completed during my Bloc apprenticeship. The project required us to design an app that allows the user to discover nearby foodtrucks, and select and purchase food items. I chose to design NomScout for the iOS 8 platform.

problem

The current app marketplace does not yet have a solution for finding and purchasing food from food trucks. Enter NomScout.

user research

To determine the essential user stories, I created a survey in which I questioned respondents about features and capabilities they would like to have in a food truck app.

I then shifted to diagramming user flows, a process which involved significant user and market research. Through personal experimentation and usage of existing food delivery apps like GrubHub and Eat24, I was able to determine a typical flow for the mobile food-ordering process, which would provide the basic structure for NomScout’s flows. Additionally, I researched established login and payment processes in apps like Facebook and Uber to ensure that NomScout’s flows encompassed the fluidity of existing mobile app patterns, but also improved where necessary.

user flows

As a pre-wireframing step, I utilized the flow diagram to create a sitemap, or appmap, which prepared me in sketching each app screen included in the user flows.

site/appmap

branding & identity

Instead of using the provided style guide and logo, I crafted an original brand for this project.

The app needed to have a recognizable brand in order to distinguish itself from the sea of current food-ordering apps (aside from having the food truck component). It also needed to possess colors that signified health, nourishment, exploration, and a bit of whimsy. I selected a shade of green as one of the primary colors for its vibrancy and correlation with freshness, particularly in the culinary world. I chose a raspberry as the action color, as its noticeable, rich hue would clearly highlight actions to be taken by the user, as well as complement the green for an appealing aesthetic. I kept the typography simple and consistent.

Keeping with the playful, adventurous tone of the brand, I chose the name "NomScout", as users would be doing just that: nom-scouting. The logo took on the form of a scout badge.

usability testing

I distributed the interactive prototype, accompanied by a simple user survey, to test NomScout’s usability. The survey instructed participants to complete specific actions, such as searching for taco trucks nearby.

Survey respondents indicated the app was straightforward, easy to use, and the instructed actions were completed quickly and without confusion.

Some features, however, like providing users with time estimates for order pick-ups, were critiqued as being difficult to develop, and were removed from the interactive prototype. I also received feedback that some of the labeling, such as the “edit order” label, would more effective if replaced by icons. I used Illustrator to draw universally recognized icons that fit with NomScout’s UI.

further exploration

NomScout has not yet undergone further exploration, but if it does, I would subject the app to usability testing following development of the prototype. More specifically, I would like to film test participants while using the app to understand immediate, organic reactions, rather than summarizing reactions with text.
